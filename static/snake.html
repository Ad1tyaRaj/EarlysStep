SNAKE BITE

A snake bite is a serious emergency that requires immediate medical attention. The venom can spread quickly, causing severe symptoms like swelling, pain, and potentially life-threatening reactions. Avoid any actions that could worsen the situation, such as cutting the wound or applying ice.

Instructions

Do Not Panic: Stay calm. Panicking can worsen the situation

Leave the Snake Alone: Snakes usually bite when they feel threatened. Maintain a safe distance and wait for professional rescuers to arrive.

Avoid Faith Healing: Refrain from attempting any traditional or faith-based remedies.

Do Not Try to Kill or Capture the Snake: Avoid any actions that might provoke the snake further.

Do Not Cut or Suck the Bite Area: These practices can cause additional harm.

Do Not Apply a Tourniquet: Tying a tourniquet can worsen tissue damage.

Do Not Apply Ice or Massage the Bitten Area: These actions can exacerbate the venom's effects.

Do Not Self-Medicate or Apply Herbs/Spices: Seek professional medical help instead
